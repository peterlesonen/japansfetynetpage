Earthquakes

Japan is located in a seismically active zone and experiences frequent earthquakes.

Before an Earthquake

  1. Secure heavy furniture to walls.
  2. Know the locations of emergency exits in buildings.
  3. Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, a flashlight, and a first-aid kit.

During an Earthquake

  1. Stay calm.
  2. If indoors, seek shelter under a sturdy table or desk, away from windows.
  3. If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, power lines, and trees.

After an Earthquake:

  1. Be prepared for aftershocks.
  2. Listen to official announcements and follow evacuation orders.

Typhoons

Typhoons, similar to hurricanes, can bring strong winds and heavy rain, especially from late summer to early autumn.

Before a typhoon

  1. Check weather forecasts regularly.
  2. Secure outdoor objects that could be blown away.
  3. Have a supply of water, food, and a flashlight.
  4. Consider taping windows to prevent shattering.

During a typhoon

  1. Stay indoors and away from windows.
  2. Avoid going outside unless necessary.
  3. If you must go outside, be cautious of flying debris and flooded areas.
  4. Follow evacuation orders from local authorities.

Tsunamis

Tsunamis can occur after strong earthquakes, especially near coastal areas.

If a tsunami warning is issued

  1. Move to higher ground immediately and stay away from the coast.
  2. Follow the instructions of local authorities.

Volcanic Eruptions

Japan has many active volcanoes.

  1. Check for volcanic activity updates and warnings.
  2. Be aware of evacuation routes and shelters.

Heatstroke

Summers in Japan can be very hot and humid, so be careful.

  1. Drink plenty of water, even if you don't feel thirsty.
  2. Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight.
  3. Wear loose-fitting, breathable clothing.
  4. Take frequent breaks in shaded or air-conditioned areas.
  5. Be aware of the symptoms of heatstroke:
    Headache
    Dizziness
    Nausea
    Confusion
    If you experience these, seek medical attention immediately.

Lost Property

Japan has an excellent lost and found system

  1. Retrace your steps and check where you last had the item.
  2. Report the loss to the nearest police station, train station, or the facility where you think you lost it (e.g., a shop, restaurant, or hotel).
  3. Provide a detailed description of the item, including any identifying features.
  4. If you lost your passport, contact your embassy or consulate immediately.
